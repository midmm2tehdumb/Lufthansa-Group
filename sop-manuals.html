1. General Procedures

Safety and Incident Reporting

Safety is the primary concern of Lufthansa. All pilots are required to report any safety-related occurrences, aircraft damage, unstable approaches, and hard landings (defined as >600fpm or 2.2Gs).

Incident reports must be submitted via the Crew Portal or to a Flight Operations Officer within 24 hours of the event.

Livery & Callsign Compliance

Pilots must use the liveries specified in the Livery Database. Standard Lufthansa (DLH) or Lufthansa Cargo (GEC) callsigns must be used. Regional partners (CityLine) should use the appropriate subsidiary prefix.

2. Weather & Hazardous Conditions

Thunderstorm & Wind Limits

  • Avoidance: Maintain 20nm distance from any cell showing red on weather radar.
  • Narrowbody Crosswind: 30 Kts limit.
  • Widebody Crosswind: 35 Kts limit.
  • Tailwind: 10 Kts maximum for all landings.

3. Flight Path Management

Standard Operating Altitudes

  • Below 10,000ft: Maximum Speed 250 KIAS.
  • Below 2,500ft: Speed 170-190 KIAS (Flaps 1 or 2).
  • Stabilized Approach: Aircraft must be stabilized (on speed, on path, configured) by 1,000ft AGL.

Bombardier CRJ-700

Takeoff & Climb

Thrust: TOGA. Rotate: 135kts. Pitch: 15° initially. Retract flaps at 180kts.

Cruise & Landing

Cruise: Mach 0.77. Approach: 140kts. Landing: Flaps 45 (Full), Vref 130kts. Note: CRJ is known for a low-profile approach; avoid excessive flare.
